This is another broad review paper. Its objective is to survey image tampering detection methods from handcrafted techniques to deep learning.

## 2. Technical Summary
Local notes characterize it as a wide survey touching traditional descriptors, CNNs, GAN-related themes, and transformer-era methods. That breadth is useful for terminology, but not necessarily for design quality. Surveys that try to mention everything often stop explaining anything in a level of detail that can drive an implementation.

The paper appears to function as a background map rather than a specific localization design reference.

## 3. Key Techniques Used
- Survey coverage from traditional features to deep learning
- Multi-technique taxonomy of tamper detection strategies
- High-level discussion of performance trends

These are useful because they help frame where a project sits in the method landscape. They are not enough to justify a concrete model without stronger supporting papers.

## 4. Strengths of the Approach
It is broad, so it can quickly expose a reader to the overall field. That is valuable when trying to understand how handcrafted and deep methods relate.

It may also help the project avoid the naive claim that only one family of technique exists.

## 5. Weaknesses or Limitations
The problem is signal-to-noise ratio. This folder already contains stronger surveys that are more directly useful. Keeping a weaker broad review in the main evidence chain mostly adds redundancy.

It is also not sharply focused on assignment-level questions such as localization outputs, hardware feasibility, and practical evaluation design.

## 6. Alignment With Assignment
Alignment: Low

The topic matches in a broad sense, but the paper is too generic to materially influence the actual project design.
