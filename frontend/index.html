How the Pipeline Works

TIDAL uses a three-stage forensic analysis pipeline. Each stage extracts different evidence of image tampering.

01

Error Level Analysis

Re-saves the image at three JPEG quality levels (Q=75, 85, 95) and measures pixel-level differences. Tampered regions show inconsistent compression artifacts across all three quality levels, producing a 9-channel forensic signature.

→
02

UNet + ResNet-34

A segmentation network with a frozen ImageNet-pretrained encoder processes the 9-channel ELA input. The conv1 layer is initialized by tiling 3ch pretrained weights 3×, enabling transfer learning from natural images to forensic features.

→
03

Tamper Localization

The model outputs a pixel-level probability map highlighting tampered regions. A threshold converts this to a binary mask, and the tampered area ratio determines the image-level classification (authentic vs. tampered).

Limitations & Precautions

🖼️ JPEG-Focused

ELA works best on JPEG images. RAW or lossless formats may produce weaker forensic signals.

📐 Fixed Resolution

Images are resized to 384×384 for analysis. Very small tampered regions may be missed.

🎯 Dataset-Specific

Trained on CASIA 2.0 (copy-move + splicing). May not generalize to all tampering methods.

🔬 Research Prototype

This is an academic investigation, not commercial forensic software.
